What is a Corporation?

A corporation is a legal entity that is entirely separate and distinct from its owners. Also occasionally called a legal person, corporations still enjoy most of the rights and responsibilities that are granted to individuals in this country.

Corporations can enter contracts on their own, loan and borrow money in their own name, sue and be sued, and hire employees. Corporations also own their assets and are independently responsible for paying their taxes.

How Do Corporations Work?

Corporations function as their own legal entity, but they still need some management. After all, a corporation can’t sign its own checks, calculate its own taxes, or manage its own investments. Corporations also certainly can’t form themselves, so you need to know what you’re getting into if you’re thinking about starting a corporation.

What is the Process like to Start a Corporation?

It’s not difficult to start a corporation, but it’s important to make sure you’re following the right process to start your corporation.

File Articles of Incorporation

Corporations are started when a group of shareholders file articles of incorporation. You can file articles of incorporation as a sole shareholder, or with a group of shareholders.

These forms generally include all the basic information about the corporation. That includes the name of the new corporation, its address, the agent for service of process, and the amount of stock to be released by that corporation.

Think of forms of incorporation as being the step that makes everything official. You should have most if not all of the details of your corporation worked out by the time you try to file articles of incorporation.

Board of Directors, Bylaws, and Day to Day Operations

Most corporations have a Board of Directors that help create the bylaws and define the day to day operations of the Corporation. This Board is usually composed of shareholders, but in the case of larger corporations, it may be limited to shareholders with a bigger controlling stake in the corporation as opposed to people with a much smaller share.

Bylaws create a legal background for the corporation to operate under, along with the original articles of incorporation. But it’s much easier to add and modify bylaws than anything included in the articles of incorporation.

Do You Need Help Starting a Corporation?

Because corporations are more complicated than LLCs and other smaller business organizations, it’s usually a good idea to get help setting up a corporation. It’s not a requirement, but having assistance will make the process much easier.

Why Would Someone Want to Start a Corporation?

There are lots of reasons you might want to start a corporation, and everyone’s reasons are a little different. Here are some of the most common reasons.

Professionalism

One of the big advantages of incorporating is that your business looks more professional to potential clients and customers as well as other businesses. Having a corporation makes it look like you know what you’re doing and increases your trustworthiness.

Limited Liability

This goes hand in hand with taxes. Corporations limit liability for their actions to the corporation itself. Shareholders, for instance, are not responsible if a corporation is in debt, and their assets (other than the value in the shares themselves) are not as risk. The same goes for the majority shareholder, founder, and any employees of the corporation.

Taxes

Having a corporation also creates a separate entity when it comes to taxes. Especially if you’re the only shareholder that can mean greater protection for your assets completely separate from the assets of the corporation, which means that your personal property isn’t at risk if your corporation runs into trouble.

Being a corporation can also give you access to different tax rates, tax breaks, and other advantages that make tax season more affordable for your business.

Privacy

Like LLCs and other business structures, corporations also offer a level of anonymity if they are created anonymously. That offers shareholder protection from the public eye, and can also help protect you from reporters and scrutiny about your business becoming personal.

Why Would Someone Want to Start a Corporation?

S Corp

S corporations are usually preferred by smaller businesses, allow up to 100 shareholders, and only offer one class of stock. They are good for corporations that want to stay small, but not for corporations that want to go public or international. These corporations also usually have a lot of upkeep.

Non-Profit

Filing and application fees are higher for non-profit organizations, and they are usually used to raise awareness or funds for an important cause or issue. Non-profit corporations have no individual ownership and have greater reporting and administrative requirements than other corporations to make sure they stay compliant will all non-profit rules and regulations.

C Corp

C corporations are a good option for bigger corporations, corporations that want to go public, and who want to have unlimited shareholders. This is the preferred corporation type if you want to bring in outside investors. It also allows shareholders to purchase an interest in any other corporation or business.

Advantages of a Corporation

Separate Legal Entity

Because corporations are a separate legal entity, they are a good option for protecting your own assets. They also pay their own taxes and offer several advantages and protections.

Easier to attract investors

Having a corporation makes it easier to attract outside investors (under some varieties of corporations), who can help raise capital for new business ventures, operational funds, and anything else you need.

Limited Liability

Limited Liability means that you, as a shareholder or the sole shareholder behind your corporation, are shielded from debt collections, taxes, and lawsuits filed against your corporation.

Easier to attract customers

Having a corporation also increases your visibility and makes it easier to attract customers and clients. As a non-profit, it’s also easier to raise awareness of your cause as well as funding.

Disadvantages of a Corporation

Maintenance is Difficult vs. LLC

Corporations take a lot more work than LLCs thanks to being a more complicated business structure. Even without investors, you should expect to spend more time on administrative tasks for your corporation.

Potential Double Taxation

While there are some potential tax advantages, corporations are also often subject to a higher tax rate. Depending on the kind of corporation you are considering forming, you may be better off with an LLC or another business structure with a lower tax burden.

Reporting to State, Investors, IRS

As a corporation, you are responsible for regular reporting to a wider group of people. The State and IRS will both require regular updates, even if you are the sole investor. You'll need to provide quarterly accountings of your business accounts, profits, and other management details. If your corporation has investors, they will also expect regular updates. Quarterly updates are usually considered the minimum, more frequent updates may be beneficial or, in some cases, required.

Should You Start a Corporation?

Who are they best for?

Corporations tend to be best for groups that are looking to raise money from outside investors, or anyone who is looking to raise awareness for a specific cause more than they are looking for personal gain. Corporations are better for people who are looking to create a larger business, as opposed to LLCs that are well designed for smaller organizations.

What is the benefit?

Corporations provide limited liability, a separate legal entity, and new fundraising opportunities compared to other business structures. They also provide a specific management style and the ability to form a board of directors to guide future management decisions.

FAQs:

What is a corporation in business?

A corporation is a distinct legal entity able to conduct business on its own behalf, including buying and selling property, making and receiving loans, owning its own accounts, and other critical rights. They are similar to legal persons, though there are still a few limitations on the rights and privileges of a corporation.

What are some examples of corporations?

Many of the big businesses you interact with every day are corporations. Businesses like Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, and Google are all corporations. Local small businesses may also be corporations, especially any that include inc. (for incorporated) in their names. If you volunteer with a non-profit, that non-profit is also a non-profit corporation.

What are the four types of corporations?

We’ve already discussed three of the four types, but here they are:

  1. C Corporations
  2. S Corporations
  3. Non-Profits
  4. Sole Proprietorships

Sole proprietorships are usually smaller, and they are easier to set up than other kinds of corporations. However, they don't offer any liability protection.
